## The Complexity of Running a Triathlon Club

Managing a triathlon club is genuinely complex. You're coordinating three sports, multiple training groups, open-water swim permits, bike safety checks, race entries, and volunteers — often with a committee of part-time volunteers.

> "The clubs that thrive are the ones that treat admin as a strategic advantage, not a necessary evil."
> — British Triathlon Club Survey, 2024

---

## Member Lifecycle Management

Every triathlete in your club goes through a predictable journey. Understanding it lets you support them better at each stage.

### The Triathlete Journey

```
DISCOVER → JOIN → TRAIN → RACE → RENEW → ADVOCATE
   |          |       |       |       |        |
 Social    Online   Group   Event   Auto-   Referral
 media     form     finder  entry   renew   programme
```

**Where most clubs lose members**: the gap between *joining* and *first training session*. A structured onboarding sequence — welcome email, intro session invite, buddy assignment — closes this gap.

## Training Group Management

### Structuring by Ability

Avoid the common mistake of grouping by discipline only. A better framework:

| Level | Swim Pace | Bike FTP | Run Pace/km | Training Focus |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Beginners** | 2:30+/100m | <200W | 6:30+ | Technique, confidence |
| **Intermediate** | 1:50–2:30 | 200–280W | 5:30–6:30 | Aerobic base, transitions |
| **Advanced** | <1:50 | 280W+ | <5:30 | Race-specific intensity |
| **Elite** | <1:30 | 320W+ | <4:30 | Periodisation, peaking |

### Session Planning Checklist

Before every training session:

- [ ] Venue booked and confirmed
- [ ] Coach/leader assigned
- [ ] Safety equipment checked (swim caps, tow floats for OWS)
- [ ] Emergency contacts list available
- [ ] Members registered on session via app
- [ ] Weather contingency plan in place

## Race Day Coordination

### Pre-Race (4 weeks out)

1. **Race entry management** — collect entries, handle waitlists, coordinate mass entries
2. **Kit check** — are members' bikes roadworthy? Helmets certified?
3. **Transport coordination** — shared minibus, car-sharing matching
4. **Volunteer sign-ups** — marshals, transition zone helpers, timing

### On the Day

The biggest stress for club coordinators is **tracking members across a race**. A simple system:

```
Race Day Tracking Sheet
─────────────────────────────────────────────────
Name         | Swim Start | T1 Out | T2 Out | Finish
─────────────────────────────────────────────────
Emma R.      |  08:15     | 09:02  | 10:18  | 11:34
James O.     |  08:15     | 09:08  | 10:31  | 11:52
Sarah M.     |  08:30     | 09:19  | 10:42  | 12:08
─────────────────────────────────────────────────
```

This is basic — but having even a paper system means you know quickly if someone hasn't emerged from the swim leg.

## Open Water Swimming: Safety Non-Negotiables

OWS is where most incident risk sits in triathlon. Minimum requirements:

- **Tow float**: every swimmer, every session
- **Safety boat or kayak**: 1 per 15 swimmers in open water
- **Cold water acclimatisation**: gradual exposure below 15°C
- **Buddy system**: no one swims alone, ever
- **Emergency action plan**: written, laminated, at the water's edge

## Technology That Actually Helps

The best club management tools for triathlon clubs share these traits:

- **Mobile-first** — coaches on the poolside don't carry laptops
- **Offline capable** — race venues often have poor signal
- **Integrated payments** — race entries, training fees, kit shop in one place
- **GDPR compliant** — especially important for medical information

### What to look for in a platform

```
Must-have features:
  ✓ Online membership registration
  ✓ Session booking with capacity limits
  ✓ Automated renewal reminders
  ✓ Event management (race entries, volunteer sign-ups)
  ✓ Communication (email + push notifications)

Nice-to-have:
  ○ Strava / Garmin integration
  ○ Performance tracking dashboards
  ○ Qualification tracking for championships
```

## Retention: Keeping Members Through Winter

Triathlon is seasonal — and winter is when clubs bleed members. Strategies that work:

- **Turbo trainer leagues**: indoor cycling competitions keep motivation high
- **Off-season swim clinics**: skill development when racing pressure is off
- **Social calendar**: quiz nights, film screenings, Christmas party
- **Goal-setting workshops**: help members plan their race season early
- **Club kit launch**: new kit drops create excitement and belonging

## Key Metrics for Triathlon Clubs

| Metric | Target | Why It Matters |
|---|---|---|
| Renewal rate | >80% | Core financial health |
| Session attendance rate | >60% | Engagement indicator |
| New member onboarding completion | >90% | Reduces early churn |
| Race entry rate | >50% of members | Validates training investment |
| Volunteer hours/member | >4 hrs/yr | Community health |
